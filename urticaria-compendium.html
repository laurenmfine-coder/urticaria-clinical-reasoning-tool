Stepwise approach to chronic urticaria management based on 2024-2025 evidence

1 Second-Generation H1-Antihistamine

If inadequate after 2-4 weeks ‚Üí Proceed to Step 2
2 Updose Antihistamine to 4√ó Standard

3 Add Biologic Therapy

Alternatives:
  • Remibrutinib ‚Äî If needle phobia, similar efficacy (oral)

4 Optimize Biologic / Consider Alternatives
Omalizumab updosing: 300mg Q2W or 450-600mg Q4W

  • Consider when biologics unavailable or failed

High-yield facts for clinical practice and board preparation

üî• High-Yield
Dupilumab has NO angioedema data ‚Äî avoid as first-line in patients with prominent angioedema despite FDA approval for CSU.
üî• High-Yield

üî• High-Yield

Pathophysiology
Benralizumab (anti-IL-5) and tezepelumab (anti-TSLP) failed because urticaria is driven by the IgE-mast cell axis, not eosinophils or TSLP.
Pathophysiology
Dupilumab works because IL-4/IL-13 are critical for IgE production ‚Äî it targets the upstream cause, not eosinophils.

Remibrutinib is BEST for angioedema (+15% more angioedema-free weeks vs +8.75% for omalizumab).
Treatment Selection
